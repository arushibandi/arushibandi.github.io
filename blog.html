Summer Reading List

By Arushi Bandi. June 13, 2016.

I used to be the kind of person who only read fiction books, but recently I've been becoming more interested in nonfiction. Sci-fi still remains my favorite genre, though. My Summer Reading List is mostly nonfiction books about a wide variety of topics, a few fiction books, and many books from Bill Gates reading list (oops) in no particular order, and I also shared the reason I wanted to read the books, in case the 0 people reading my blog wanted to know.

  1. Seveneves by Neal Stephenson I got this book off of Bill Gates' reading list and finished it last week, so technically it's not on my reading list anymore. I loved the characters and the plot twist at the end and being the geeky person I am, loved the technicalities about physics/space.
  2. How Not to Be Wrong: The Power of Mathematical Thinking by Jordan Ellenberg Because I think I'm right alot of th time, and just want to make sure. This book is also from Bill Gates' reading list.
  3. The Mind Club: Who Thinks, What Feels, and Why It Matters by Daniel M. Wegner and Kurt Gray Reading about Psychology is fun.
  4. Moonwalking with Einstein: The Art and Science of Remembering Everything Self-improvement books are good, especially when they can help you remember nonsensical facts for upcoming tests.
  5. West with the Night by Beryl Markham Because strong women setting records is cool.
  6. Accelerando (Singularity) by Charles Stross So a couple months ago, I was reading a book recommended to me by my aunt (Mr. Penumbra's 24-hour Bookstore by Robin Sloan - great book by the way, also the cover glows in the dark, which is +10) and one of the characters talked about an idea called singularity and I loved her and thought her idea was cool, so I added this to my reading list. I also like reading about what other people predict to be the future of the human race.
  7. The Tipping Point by Malcolm Gladwell I like thinking of ideas, and learning about how to think of ideas.
  8. What the Dog Saw by Malcolm Gladwell I started reading Blink and got about half-way through before the quarter ended and I had to write a book report on it, so I never finished it. Also reading nonfiction is hard sometimes. But I was interested in the topics Gladwell talked about, and this book has been described as a sort of summary of his others, so I put it on here.
  9. The Vital Question: Energy, Evolution and the Origins of Complex Life by Nick Lane Guess where I got this? If you guessed Bill Gates' Reading list, you're right.
  10. Change by Design: How Design Thinking Transforms Organizations and Inspires Innovation by Tim Brown I'm one of those people whose dream is to come up with a great idea and found a start-up, so yeah, this book appealed to me.
  11. The Sixth Extinction: An Unnatural History by Elizabeth Kolbert Honestly, the reason I want to read this is because I read the advertising hook thing to it and I just wanted to know what the sixth extinction is and it didn't say, so now I have to find out.
  12. The Charisma Myth: How Anyone Can Master the Art and Science of Personal Magnetism by Olivia Fox Cabane Because who doesn't want to be liked by everyone?
  13. The Rosie Project: A Novel by Graeme Simsion I've never been a fan of romance novels or anything cheesy for that matter, so at first I didn't want to read this book, but what can I say? Bill Gates convinced me.
  14. The Clay Lion by Amalie Jahn This book seems like one I'd have loved as a kid because time traveling!! I can't wait to read the series as well.
  15. Atlas Shrugged by Ayn Rand I had to read Anthem by Ayn Rand sophomore year and to be honest, while I understand the point she tried to make, I felt as if I could have understood the moral just as easily if it was stated in one sentence. But I like reading people's views about human existence, so I decided to give it a try
  16. Sapiens: A Brief History of Human Kind by Yuval Noah Harari This seems like the kind of book my parents would read. I've heard so many different opinions from people about the transition from simple to complex biological life, so I wanted to learn more about it for myself.
  17. The Atlantis Gene by A. G. Riddle I love books where there's a couple people trying to save the whole world, and the basis of this seemed not too far-fetched, just enough to keep me interested.

I'm not sure how many of these I will make it through in the next three months, but I'm gonna try my best!
