A random collection of sources and Internet links

  • tech demise - an amazing Are.na channel with a ton of journalism on the tragic and weird way techno-capitalism restructures our world

A slightly less random assortment of books

  • The Real World of Technology by Ursula M. Franklin. Absolutely necessary.
  • The Software Arts by Warren Sacks. Illuminates how computing stems less from logic and more from rhetoric & the language arts than we might think. Useful for parsing "demos".
  • The Age of Surveillance Captilaism. Dense, technical, long, but worth it.
  • The Gutenberg Elegies, not super well known but a book that kickstarted a lot of thinking for me.
  • FSG Originals x Logic Magazine Series. Short, to the point, and incredible insightful.
  • How to do Nothing and of course Saving Time by Jenny Odell. Will read anything with her name on it.
  • Trick Mirror by Jia Tolentino. Not exactly focused on the technology, but on the feeling of it all, and of growing up in the 21st century quite well.
  • How to Resist Amazon and Why by Danny Caine, owner of Raven Bookstore in Kansas City. If you liked Berry, you will like this.

  • The Parable of the Sower by Octavia Butler
